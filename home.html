ATLANTIS

Atlantis will celebrate its 50th anniversary in 2026. To commemorate this milestone, a series of community events and activities will be coordinated under the banner called “Celebrate Atlantis”. This website was created and will be dedicated to celebrate the many successes and infinite talent this town has to offer. The website will also promote all the initiatives planned for 2026.

Celebrate Atlantis is a dynamic, multi-platform initiative designed to showcase and honour the rich tapestry of our town's diverse offerings. Through engaging content across social media, digital channels, and community platforms, it aims to bring together the voices, stories, and achievements that make our town unique - from our thriving local businesses and innovative spirit, to the many talented people excelling in their respective fields.

Throughout 2026 and beyond this digital movement aims to create a vibrant, inclusive space where residents, former residents, and visitors can connect, share memories, and discover the many facets that make Atlantis a truly special place to live, work, and grow.

A working group of residents with the necessary expertise will soon be established to manage a range of positive initiatives and celebrations planned throughout 2026.

Soon we will share more information on this platform of how YOU could become involved.

Atlantis turns 50: Interested parties asked to serve on voluntary task team

Atlantis will celebrate its 50th anniversary in 2026. To commemorate this milestone, a series of community events and activities will be coordinated under the banner called “Celebrate Atlantis”.

As part of this initiative, a small task team needs to be established to guide and coordinate ideas, events, and proposals under this banner. This task team will play a central role in ensuring that the anniversary activities are inclusive and community-driven. The exact scope and responsibilities of the task team will be determined at the first meeting, which will be held at the ASEZ offices in Atlantis in late October or early November. The date and time of the meeting will be confirmed in due course.

It is important to note that there are no commitments or promises to fund or financially support any projects or events. Those interested in attending the initial meeting are invited to send the following information to info@celebrateatlantis.co.za.

  • A short professional introduction,
  • A summary of their skill set, and
  • E-mail address and mobile number

This will determine suitability and avoid duplication of skills. No ideas, project proposals, or concept documents should be shared at this early stage. A dedicated website, www.celebrateatlantis.co.za, will soon host regular updates and information about the initiative.

Any personal information submitted will be used solely for purposes related to this initiative. All data will be handled in accordance with the Protection of Personal Information Act (POPIA) and will not be shared with third parties without consent.

1975

Founding & Early Settlement

Founding & Early Settlement

The first settlement of Atlantis in the Western Cape began with planned housing and industrial development that shaped the town's early character and laid the groundwork for its future growth.

This initial phase was marked by significant public investment aimed at creating a self-sufficient town with a strong economic base.

Community Growth

Community Growth

Over the following decade, the town expanded as new housing and community services were introduced, fostering a strong and vibrant local identity among its growing population of residents.

Schools, clinics, and recreational facilities were established, turning the area from a settlement into a fully-fledged community with a unique cultural fabric.

Industry & Change

Industry & Change

Industrial shifts and economic changes significantly impacted employment patterns, but these challenges also laid the crucial foundations for future revitalisation initiatives that would redefine the town's economic landscape.

The community demonstrated remarkable resilience during this period, adapting to new economic realities and strengthening local support networks.

Revitalisation Efforts

Revitalisation Efforts

Local and national programs in the 2000s focused on critical infrastructure, workforce training, and strategic investment to support a greener, more resilient, and sustainable future for all.

A key focus was the development of a green technology hub, attracting new industries and creating opportunities for a new generation of skilled workers.
